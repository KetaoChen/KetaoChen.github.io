## 2. [2026 International AI Safety Report Released](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/2026-international-ai-safety-report-charts-rapid-changes-and-emerging-risks-302677298.html)

The 2026 International AI Safety Report, chaired by Turing Award-winner Yoshua Bengio and backed by over 30 countries, was released today. Key findings include: AI systems achieved gold-medal performance on International Math Olympiad questions and exceeded PhD-level performance on science benchmarks; at least 700 million people now use leading AI systems weekly; deepfake incidents are rising, with AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery increasingly common; and malicious actors actively use AI in cyberattacks, with underground marketplaces selling pre-packaged AI tools.

**Why it matters:** The report provides decision-makers with rigorous evidence to steer AI development, highlighting both rapid capability improvements and emerging risks that require stronger safeguards.

*Published: February 3, 2026 (PR Newswire)*

## 3. [Palantir Beats Q4 Earnings on Strong AI and Defense Demand](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/02/palantir-pltr-q4-2025-earnings.html)

Palantir reported Q4 earnings of 25 cents per share (vs. 23 cents expected) and revenue of $1.41 billion (vs. $1.33 billion expected), representing 70% year-over-year growth. The AI-powered software provider's fiscal 2026 revenue guidance of $7.18-7.20 billion implies 61% topline growth, crushing Wall Street's 43% estimate. CEO Alex Karp called the results "indisputably the best results that I'm aware of in tech in the last decade." U.S. commercial customers grew from 14 five years ago to 571 today.

**Why it matters:** Palantir's explosive growth demonstrates surging enterprise demand for AI tools, with the company's AIP bootcamp model enabling businesses and government agencies to rapidly deploy AI solutions.

*Published: February 2, 2026 (CNBC)*

## 4. [ChatGPT Suffers Brief Outage](https://www.techradar.com/news/live/chatgpt-open-ai-down-outage-february-3-2026)

ChatGPT experienced a disruption on February 3, 2026, with many users reporting issues and OpenAI confirming "elevated error rates" for both fine-tuning jobs and platform users. The outage appeared more severe outside the United States. OpenAI quickly identified the issues and applied mitigations, with service recovering within a few hours. Reports on Down Detector showed a spike in complaints before dropping as the platform recovered.

**Why it matters:** The outage highlights the growing dependency on AI platforms as hundreds of millions of users worldwide rely on ChatGPT for daily tasks, making service reliability increasingly critical.

*Published: February 3, 2026 (TechRadar)*
