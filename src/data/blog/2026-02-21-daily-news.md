## 1. [Anthropic Launches Claude Code Security for AI-Powered Vulnerability Scanning](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/anthropic-launches-claude-code-security.html)

Anthropic has rolled out Claude Code Security, a new tool that autonomously scans codebases for security vulnerabilities and suggests targeted software patches for human review. Available as a limited research preview for Claude Enterprise and Teams users, the company also plans to offer expedited access to open-source project maintainers.

**Why it matters:** Cybersecurity stocks dropped on the news, signaling that AI-native security tools could disrupt the traditional static analysis market.

*Published: February 20-21, 2026*

## 2. [NYT: People Loved the Dot-Com Boom. The AI Boom, Not So Much.](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/21/technology/ai-boom-backlash.html)

The New York Times reports that tech leaders are growing worried about the public's underwhelming enthusiasm for AI. Despite promises that AI will be "bigger than fire," surveys show over a third of Americans fear AI could end human life, and most say they wouldn't pay extra for AI features.

**Why it matters:** Public skepticism could slow AI adoption and invite heavier regulation, challenging the industry's trillion-dollar investment thesis.

*Published: February 21, 2026*

## 3. [Sanders Warns US "Not a Clue" About Coming AI Revolution](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/feb/21/ai-revolution-bernie-sanders-warning)

Speaking at Stanford alongside Rep. Ro Khanna after meetings with Silicon Valley leaders, Bernie Sanders called this "the most dangerous moment in modern history" and renewed his call for a moratorium on AI data center expansion. Khanna pushed back on the moratorium but proposed a "Singapore model" for data center growth focused on renewable energy.

**Why it matters:** Bipartisan concern about AI's pace is growing, with concrete policy proposals now on the table from both progressive and centrist camps.

*Published: February 21, 2026*

## 4. [Tech Giants Commit Hundreds of Billions to Indian AI at Impact Summit](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/21/india-ai-summit-tech-giants-billion-dollar-investments.html)

At the India AI Impact Summit in New Delhi, Reliance announced $110B in AI data center investment, Adani outlined $100B over the next decade, and Microsoft committed $50B in AI investment in the Global South by end of decade. OpenAI and AMD partnered with Tata Group. The summit was also marked by controversy, including Bill Gates's withdrawal over Epstein backlash.

**Why it matters:** India is positioning itself as a major AI hub, and the sheer scale of commitments signals a geographic shift in AI infrastructure beyond the US and China.

*Published: February 21, 2026*
